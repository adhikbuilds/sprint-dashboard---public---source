Hey Team — Context & Why We're Here

Hey guys, I'm Adhik — the project lead for Personal Health. Thanks for joining this sprint.

Quick context: we're building a phone-first sports biomechanics AI platform. Athletes train using their phone camera, we grade form in real-time, track heart rate via rPPG, and over time build a model on real session data. The codebase is already functional — we have 30 seeded athletes, 600+ sessions, working pose analysis, leaderboard, daily tracker, social feed, the whole thing.

We have two domains:

  • Personal Fitness / Biomechanics — This is our core. Pose analysis, form scoring, rep counting. We're keeping this on hold for now because we need more real athlete data before the next model retrain makes sense.
  • Health & Wellness — This is where we're sprinting. Nutrition tracking, enhanced wellness monitoring, sleep analysis, recovery scoring, meal logging. This is the focus of this sprint.

The daily tracker already exists (water, sleep, mood, soreness) but it's bare-minimum. We need to build it out into a proper wellness system that athletes actually use daily. The nutrition side is completely greenfield — nothing exists yet.

What I expect from each of you: Read through your assigned user stories carefully. Understand the acceptance criteria — that's your contract. Every PR goes through Raj Shukla for peer review. Don't build beyond the story scope, but do think about edge cases. If something doesn't make sense technically, flag it in standup — don't guess.

Domains this sprint: Fitness module is on hold (needs 500+ real sessions for meaningful retrain). Wellness & Nutrition is the sprint focus. All new code follows existing patterns in api_server.py, HomeScreen.js, and server.js.

Section 03 — Product Framework

Jobs-to-be-Done Analysis

Using the JTBD framework (from Product-Manager-Skills/skills/jobs-to-be-done), here's what our athletes are actually hiring the wellness module to do:

Customer Jobs

Functional Jobs

  • Track what I eat so I know if I'm fueling my training properly — not guessing
  • Log my sleep and recovery so I can decide whether today is a push day or a rest day
  • See my wellness trend over a week/month — am I getting healthier or running myself into the ground?
  • Hit my nutrition targets — know my protein/carb/fat breakdown without a nutritionist
  • Understand why my form dips — is it sleep? nutrition? stress? Show me the correlation

Social Jobs

  • Show my coach I'm serious — I track everything, not just training reps
  • Prove to my parents that I'm eating well and sleeping enough during tournament prep
  • Be seen as disciplined by peers — my wellness streak shows commitment

Emotional Jobs

  • Feel in control of my training — not just showing up and grinding blindly
  • Avoid the guilt of not knowing if I ate right before a competition
  • Feel confident that rest days are backed by data, not laziness
  • Reduce anxiety about overtraining — the app tells me when to back off

Pains

Pain TypeDescription
ChallengeNo affordable tool combines nutrition + wellness + training performance in one place for Indian athletes. They use 3 different apps or nothing at all.
ChallengeFood databases are Western-centric. Indian athletes eat poha, dal-rice, roti-sabzi — not "chicken breast and broccoli." Existing apps make logging a chore.
CostlinessA sports nutritionist costs ₹2,000–5,000/session. Athletes at Block/District tier can't afford monthly nutrition guidance.
Common MistakeAthletes skip meals before training, overeat carbs post-session, or ignore hydration — because nobody taught them the basics.
UnresolvedNo tool shows how last night's sleep directly correlates with today's form score. The data exists in separate silos.

Gains

Gain TypeDescription
ExpectationLog a meal in under 30 seconds — search "dal rice", tap, done. No typing quantities manually.
SavingsReplace a ₹3,000/month nutritionist with an app that's free and knows Indian food natively.
AdoptionIf it works with the training data I already have (form scores, sessions), I don't need to start from scratch.
Life ImprovementKnow whether to train hard or rest — before I leave the house. One screen, one number, one decision.

WN-08   3 SP
Enhanced Wellness Logging — Full Daily Entry
As an athlete, I want to log detailed wellness data each morning (sleep quality, hydration, mood, stress, energy, soreness, body weight), so that I have a complete picture of my daily readiness.
Acceptance Criteria
Given a POST to /athlete/{id}/wellness with body:
{"date": "2026-04-07", "sleep": {"bedtime": "23:15", "wake_time": "06:45", "quality": 4, "interruptions": 1}, "hydration": {"water_ml": 2800}, "mental": {"mood": 7, "stress": 4, "energy": 7, "journal_note": "..."}, "physical": {"soreness": 3, "body_weight_kg": 68.5}}
Then the entry is saved to db/wellness.json under athlete_id → date
And sleep.duration_hrs is computed from bedtime/wake_time (handle midnight crossing)
And sleep.sleep_score is computed (formula below)
And hydration.target_ml is computed as body_weight_kg × 35 (ml), hydration.pct derived
And wellness_score (0–100 composite) is computed and stored
And recovery_ready (boolean) is computed: true if wellness_score >= 60
And logged_at timestamp is added
And re-POST for the same date overwrites the previous entry
And partial body is accepted — only provided fields update; missing fields retain previous values or null
Sleep Score Formula:
base = (duration_hrs / 8.0) × 40   // max 40 pts for 8+ hrs
quality_pts = quality × 8   // max 40 pts (quality 1-5)
interruption_penalty = interruptions × 5   // -5 per interruption
sleep_score = clamp(base + quality_pts - interruption_penalty, 0, 100)

Wellness Score Formula (composite):
sleep_component = sleep_score × 0.30
hydration_component = hydration_pct × 0.15
mood_component = (mood / 10) × 100 × 0.15
energy_component = (energy / 10) × 100 × 0.15
stress_component = ((10 - stress) / 10) × 100 × 0.10   // inverted: low stress = good
soreness_component = ((10 - soreness) / 10) × 100 × 0.15   // inverted
wellness_score = round(sum of components)
Raj's Review Checklist: Test midnight crossing (bedtime 23:30, wake 06:00 → 6.5hrs). Test all-5s input → should be high score. Test all-1s → low score. Test partial update. Verify _save_db(). Test negative body weight → 400.

Brownie Features — Deeper Product Scope Bonus

These are not required for this sprint but are logical extensions that would differentiate the product. If a dev finishes early, these are the stretch goals — pick one, scope it, and propose it in standup.

BF-01: AI Meal Suggestions Based on Training Load

When an athlete has a session scheduled or completed, suggest meals that match their caloric needs for that day. E.g., "You burned ~450 cal in your sprint session. Consider adding a protein-rich snack: paneer tikka (280 cal, 22g protein)."

Why it matters: Closes the loop between training and nutrition — no other Indian sports app does this.

Technical scope: New endpoint /athlete/{id}/nutrition/suggestions that reads today's session data + nutrition summary + goals → generates 2–3 food suggestions from db/foods.json.

BF-02: Meal Photo Logging (Camera → Approximate Calories)

Athletes take a photo of their plate. The backend uses a simple heuristic (or future ML model) to estimate the food type and portion. MVP: just attach the photo to the meal log entry for coach review.

Why it matters: Reduces logging friction from 30 seconds to 5 seconds. Photos create a richer dataset for future food recognition ML.

Technical scope: Use expo-camera to capture, upload base64 to POST /athlete/{id}/meals with an optional photo_b64 field. Store as reference — no ML needed for MVP.

BF-03: Hydration Reminders via Push Notifications

If an athlete's hydration is below 50% of target by 2 PM, send a push notification: "You've had 1.2L of 3L today. Drink up!"

Why it matters: Dehydration is the #1 preventable performance reducer in Indian climate. Proactive nudges drive habit formation.

Technical scope: Expo push notifications + a lightweight cron/check on the backend.

BF-04: Intermittent Fasting Tracker

Some athletes follow 16:8 fasting. Track the eating window, show a countdown timer to next meal window, log fasting hours.

Why it matters: Growing trend among amateur athletes. Differentiates from generic calorie counters.

Technical scope: Add fasting_config to nutrition goals (window_start, window_end). Display timer on NutritionScreen.

BF-05: Supplement Tracking

Track daily supplement intake: whey protein, creatine, multivitamin, BCAA, etc. Show compliance streaks.

Why it matters: Athletes spend ₹1,000–3,000/month on supplements but don't track consistency. Coaches want to know if athletes are actually taking what's prescribed.

Technical scope: New section in wellness log. Simple checkbox list per day with pre-configured supplement list.

BF-06: Sleep Quality Prediction

After 30+ days of data, predict tonight's expected sleep quality based on today's training load, stress, and caffeine intake. "Based on your hard session and stress level, you might sleep poorly tonight. Consider winding down early."

Why it matters: Proactive coaching is more valuable than reactive logging. This turns data into foresight.

Technical scope: Simple linear model in wellness_engine.py using historical patterns.

BF-07: Wellness Streaks & Achievements

Track consecutive days of wellness logging. Unlock achievements: "7-Day Streak", "30-Day Warrior", "Hydration Hero (hit target 7 days straight)." Feed into the existing XP/achievement system in UserContext.js.

Why it matters: Gamification drives daily engagement. The hardest part of wellness tracking is building the habit — streaks help.

Technical scope: Computed server-side when wellness entry is saved. Add to existing achievement system.

BF-08: Recipe Suggestions Based on Dietary Preferences

If an athlete is vegetarian and low on protein, suggest recipes: "Try paneer bhurji (32g protein per serving) or sprouted moong salad (18g)." Link to a simple recipe view with ingredients and prep steps.

Positioning Statement — Wellness Module

For young Indian athletes who train without a nutritionist,
Personal Health Wellness is a phone-first wellness tracker
that combines nutrition logging, sleep analysis, and recovery scoring with training performance data
so that every morning, you know whether to push hard or rest — and every meal, you know if you're fueling your goals or undermining them.

Unlike MyFitnessPal or HealthifyMe, which are generic calorie counters with Western food databases,
Personal Health knows Indian food natively, connects nutrition to your actual form scores, and gives you a single wellness number that means something to an athlete — not just a consumer.

Mobile UI Rules (from UI/UX Pro Max)

  • No emojis as structural icons. Use @expo/vector-icons (Ionicons, MaterialIcons) for all icons. Emojis are OK in journal notes or user-generated content, never in navigation or status indicators.
  • Touch targets: Minimum 44×44pt for all tappable elements. Use hitSlop if the visual element is smaller.
  • Tap feedback: Every button/pressable must have opacity feedback (0.7 on press). Use Pressable with style={({pressed}) => ...} pattern.
  • Spacing rhythm: Use 4/8/12/16/24/32 px increments. No arbitrary spacing values.
  • Safe areas: Respect top/bottom safe areas. Use SafeAreaView or useSafeAreaInsets() on all screens.
  • Animation timing: Micro-interactions 150–300ms with ease-out easing. No instant transitions, no slow animations (>500ms).
  • Text contrast: Primary text >= 4.5:1 ratio against dark backgrounds (our palette already meets this).
  • Disabled states: Use 40% opacity + non-interactive for disabled buttons. Never use a grayed-out button that still responds to taps.

Component Patterns to Follow

  • Progress rings: Use react-native-svg with Circle + strokeDasharray/strokeDashoffset. See MetricsScreen.js spider chart for existing SVG usage.
  • Cards: borderRadius: 12, padding: 16, backgroundColor: '#111827', borderWidth: 1, borderColor: '#1e293b'.
  • Section headers: 11px, uppercase, letter-spacing 1.5, color #06b6d4 — matching existing MetricsScreen.js pattern.
  • Loading states: Use ActivityIndicator with color #06b6d4. Never show a blank screen while loading.
  • Error states: Show error message in a card with #ef4444 border-left. Include a "Retry" button.
  • Empty states: Centered illustration text + CTA button. Never show a blank screen.
